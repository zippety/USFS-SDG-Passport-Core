## 2. The "Symbiotic Loop" (The Flow)

This is the operational cycle of how money, data, and users flow between them.

### Step 1: The "Trojan Horse" (Engagement)
*   **Action:** USFS Club hosts an "SDG Gamification Hackathon" or "Wellness Challenge" on campus.
*   **Funding:** USFS applies for SSF club funding ($200â€“$500) or Service Corps grants (via SDG Labs) to buy pizza and prizes.

### Step 2: The "Validation" (Data)
*   **Action:** 100 students use the app for a month.
*   **Benefit to SDG Labs:** You get a report saying, "We engaged 100 youth in SDG action." (Great for grant reports).
*   **Benefit to Agape Home:** You get 100 beta testers, bug reports, and user data to prove to investors that "students love this."

### Step 3: The "Upsell" (Revenue)
*   **Action:** You take the report from Step 2 to the Seneca Dean or a corporate sponsor.
*   **The Pitch:** "Look, 500 students are already tracking their carbon footprint. You (the School) should buy the **Administrator Dashboard** to see this data live."
*   **The Sale:** **Agape Home** sells the dashboard license to the school.

## 3. Avoiding "Self-Dealing" (The Firewall)

To keep this "flow" legal, you must respect the boundaries:

### Rule 1: USFS Club is NOT your Company
*   **The Law:** The club cannot *buy* your software. It should get it for **free**.
*   **Why?** SSF rules strictly forbid clubs from "entering contracts" or "generating personal gain" for executives. If you try to sell your app to your own club, the SSF will shut you down.
*   **Correct Flow:** Agape Home *donates* the license to USFS. The "payment" is the exposure and user testing, not cash.

### Rule 2: SDG Labs is the "Fiscal Sponsor"
*   If USFS wants to apply for a big grant (e.g., $5,000) but lacks the legal status, **SDG Labs** acts as the partner.
*   **Flow:** SDG Labs receives the money -> buys supplies/food -> USFS hosts the event.
